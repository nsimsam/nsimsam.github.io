Noor Simsam

Medical Student

Noor Simsam is a Syrian-Canadian Muslim medical student working at the intersection of equity and innovation, drawing on biomedical engineering, AI, and bioinformatics to improve care in underserved settings and for historically marginalized communities. Having emigrated from Syria, she witnessed firsthand the impact of war on healthcare systems, which shaped her interest in frugal biomedical engineering and open-source technology — an interest she later deepened through her MSc thesis on diagnostic inequity and her patient advocacy work.

She has served as a global fellow with the Institute for Healthcare Improvement, where she led a quality improvement initiative to introduce 3D-printed otoscopes in a Pacific Island nation. She co-founded the Canadian Muslim Women in Medicine Network (CMWIM), advancing mentorship and representation for Muslim women in medicine, and her advocacy for racialized youth and Syrian refugees has been recognized with Leadership and Community Service awards.

Outside of work, she enjoys tinkering with tech, DIY projects, nature biking, and competitive Uno with family.
